This timeline shows a short history of motor bikes from the past and a glimpse into the future

1884

Lucius Day Copeland was a pioneering 19th-century engineer and inventor from Phoenix, Arizona who demonstrated one of the first motorcycles, the Copeland steam bicycle, a steam-powered Star high-wheeler at the first Arizona Territorial Fair in 1884

1900

Triumph Motorcycles Model H. Ths Bike was one of the first mass produced motor cycles and was notable for its reliability

1920

This is a classic Norton motorcycle from the 1920's

1950

This is a AJS 500cc Model 18 1950's era motorcycle. It was also the last of the big British sinlges, after this production was concertrated on twins.

1970

This bike from the 1970's is the Honda GL 1000 Goldwing. It has a flat 4 cylider 999cc engine.

1990

This bike from the 1990's is a Honda CB-1. It has a 399cc engine and is considered a naked bike due to the lack of ferrings and other more common fixtures.

2010

This bike from the 2010's is a Yamaha XV1900A. The engine in this bike is a massive 1854cc engine and is made in America.

Concept/Future

This is the Arc Vector Electric concept bike which has made it to limited production. 355 units are being sold ad $114,000 each. This is an all electric bike and is the future of all motor vechiles.
